What Are SMART Tiers?

SMART Tiers is a classification system used to categorise SMART form requests based on their overall complexity.

Each SMART form is assigned a SMART Score, which is calculated by evaluating the implementation complexity, workflow sophistication, user value, and business value of the functions used within the form.

Understanding SMART Complexity

SMART form complexity is determined by the combination of functions used within the overall workflow.

Some SMART functions provide foundational functionality with minimal implementation complexity, while others introduce advanced automation, validation, calculations, integrations, routing logic, or conditional workflow behaviour.

As additional scored functionality is introduced into a form, the overall SMART Score increases, which may result in a higher SMART Tier classification.

This approach helps ensure:

Delivery expectations remain proportionate to complexity
Appropriate implementation planning can take place
Clinical governance and testing requirements are considered

How SMART Tiers Work

SMART forms are classified using a cumulative SMART scoring framework. Each SMART function contributes differently toward the overall complexity of a form based on factors such as:

Implementation Complexity
Workflow Sophistication
User Value
Business Value
SMART functions are grouped into two categories
Essential Functions

Base-level functionality that supports core referral workflows without increasing overall SMART complexity.

Scored Functions

Advanced functionality that contributes toward the overall SMART Score and may increase the form's tier classification.

The more scored functionality included within a form, the greater the overall workflow complexity may become.

Important Note — SMART scoring is influenced by:
Overall workflow design
Functionality combinations
Configuration dependencies
Integration requirements
Clinical governance considerations

A single functionality alone does not automatically determine a form's final SMART Tier classification. Some workflows may appear simple on the surface, but the combination of validation, calculations, routing, conditional logic, integrations, automation, and clinical requirements can significantly increase the overall complexity of a referral form. Final SMART classification is assessed collaboratively by DXS during the specification and review process.

SMART Tier Definitions

The three SMART Tiers are outlined below.

Lower complexity

Essential

Forms that pre-populate details from the patient record, but are primarily designed to capture information efficiently, similar to a standard clinical template. These forms have a lower overall level of complexity and may include some SMART functionality where appropriate, but do not require extensive automation, advanced decision-making, or complex workflow configuration.

Moderate Complexity

Enhanced

Forms with a moderate level of complexity that include additional SMART functionality requiring further assessment, configuration and testing. These forms often introduce increased automation, validation, guidance, or workflow support beyond standard information capture.

High Complexity

Elite

Highly sophisticated forms with a high level of complexity that require advanced SMART functionality, extensive configuration, testing and validation. These forms typically include multiple layers of automation, complex workflow logic, advanced validation, calculations, integrations, or decision-support capabilities.

SMART Tiers are determined by the overall complexity of the form and its requirements. The presence of a specific feature does not automatically determine a form's tier.

Summary
In Summary

Referral content development is iterative, with requirements often refined through testing, feedback, and real-world use. An Essential form is a fully functional referral pathway that delivers the agreed requirements and value from day one. SMART Tiers provide a framework for classifying requests based on the complexity of the functionality and workflow involved, helping to set clear delivery expectations. The objective is to deliver the right level of functionality for the pathway. SMART Tiers reflect complexity, not quality or completeness.
